"So by adding charcoal or sulfur to an oxide you can heat it to become a metal but by adding nitre or maybe hydroxide to a metal and heating it, the metal will oxidize?" -Me

"Yes, that's correct! By adding a reducing agent like charcoal or sulfur to a metal oxide and heating it, you can perform a reduction reaction, which removes oxygen from the metal oxide and results in the formation of a pure metal. This process is known as smelting. On the other hand, by adding an oxidizing agent like nitre (saltpeter) or potassium hydroxide (potash) to a metal and heating it, you can perform an oxidation reaction, which adds oxygen to the metal and forms an oxide. This process is known as roasting or calcination. In both cases, the redox reaction involves the transfer of electrons between the reactants, which results in the transformation of the original substance into a new form. The specific conditions, such as temperature, atmosphere, and choice of reactants, can be adjusted to control the outcome of the redox reaction and achieve the desired alchemical transformation." - GabAI
