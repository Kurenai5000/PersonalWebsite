"Organic metal compounds, also known as organometallic compounds, are compounds that contain at least one metal-carbon bond. These compounds combine the properties of both organic and inorganic chemistry, allowing for a wide range of applications in various fields, including catalysis, materials science, and medicine. There are several types of organic metal compounds, which can be classified based on their structure, the metal involved, and the type of ligands attached to the metal center. Here are some common types of organic metal compounds:

Metal carbonyls: These compounds consist of a metal center bonded to one or more carbonyl (CO) ligands. Examples include nickel carbonyl (Ni(CO)4) and iron pentacarbonyl (Fe(CO)5).

Metal alkyls and aryls: These compounds contain a metal center bonded to one or more alkyl (e.g., methyl, ethyl) or aryl (e.g., phenyl) groups. Examples include tetramethyllead (Pb(CH3)4) and ferrocene (Fe(C5H5)2).

Metal cyclopentadienyl complexes (metallocenes): These compounds consist of a metal center sandwiched between one or more cyclopentadienyl (Cp) ligands. Examples include ferrocene (Fe(C5H5)2) and titanocene dichloride (Ti(C5H5)2Cl2).

Metal phosphine complexes: These compounds contain a metal center coordinated to one or more phosphine ligands (PR3). Examples include Wilkinson's catalyst (RhCl(PPh3)3) and Vaska's complex (IrCl(CO)(PPh3)2).

Metal carbene complexes: These compounds contain a metal center bonded to a carbene ligand, which is a divalent carbon species with two unshared electrons. Examples include Grubbs' catalyst (RuCl2(PCy3)2(CHPh)) and Schrock's catalyst (Mo(CH-t-Bu)(NAr)[OCMe(CF3)2]2).

Metal arene complexes: These compounds consist of a metal center coordinated to an aromatic ring, such as benzene or toluene. Examples include chromium hexacarbonyl (Cr(CO)6) and bis(benzene)chromium (Cr(C6H6)2).

Metal hydrides: These compounds contain a metal center bonded to one or more hydrogen atoms. Examples include lithium aluminum hydride (LiAlH4) and sodium borohydride (NaBH4)." - Gab AI
