Measurement

Measurement is a great art to know as it gives us a tool to understand how long or short something is and compare it to other things. Combined with geometry it lets us virtualise shapes on a computer and know our clothing sizes.
It is a fundamental field of study to understand the physical world. Though even the energy world can be measured with temperature, pressure and electromagnetism.

We can measure physical amounts of things, liquid volumes, temperature, moisture, vibration, frequency, physical areas of spaces, weight, length of lines and curves and the size of points or pixels.
Measurement is usually done at a standard, human level. Though, you can measure things in small, very small or incredibly small scales. Similarly, measurement can be done on giant things.

Metric system

The metric system is system tied to x10, x100, x1000, etc. 10 milimeters equals 1 centimeter, 100 centimeters equals 1 meter. It's a simple system but lacks relation to reality which the imerial system has.

Fluids

Common fluid measurements are litres which is a small jug of water as the middle standard and milileters. A millilitre is around a couple drops of water/fluid. 1000 millilitres makes a litre.

Distance

The middle ground for distance is the meter symbolised by m, it's around the distance from your finger to the other shoulder or so.
Kilometeres kilo meaning thousand, symbolised by km are a larger range of distance. Maybe how much you would walk on a short walk. 1000 meters makes a kilometer.
Centimeters, symbolised by cm are useful for trades work. Making furnature for example is one good use of them. A table might be 140 cm long.
Milimeters, symbolised by mm are good for fine measurement, precise 3d printing or precision engineering. 10mm makes 1 cm.
